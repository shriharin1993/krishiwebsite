Huh ! Artificial Intelligence? What happened to Natural Intelligence?

The other day, my wife who is a child psychologist enquiringly asked how was my progress with my AI/ML course. She casually asked why the world is gung ho about this new thing called Artificial Intelligence instead of worrying about Human Natural Intelligence which was getting wasted. A few days back even a good friend of mine, an analog electronics wiz during engineering school days (the only guy in our batch who understood Millman and Halkeas) also had made a similar comment about Natural intelligence vs. Artificial Intelligence.

Most of the principles being used in AI/ML have been around 25-40 years (if not earlier). I myself remember fiddling with Neural networks using opamps, resistors, capacitors; trying to construct a hardwired neural network for doing A to D conversion (and miserably failing at that), during my Engineering project days more than 25 years ago. For the common man (even an Engineer), somehow the word 'Artificial' makes one generally think that it is somehow synthetic or fake - like an artificial leg, denture, or things like astro turf or a water reservoir. Of course, news about Sophia the robot (who is powered by AI) makes people think AI is an alternate way of thinking and very soon, AI will replace Human intelligence. The news and media stories on how AI is reducing manual jobs in terms of data analysis, tabulation, business decision making seems to make one naturally nervous and apprehensive about AI.

However, I feel the word 'Artificial' could have been replaced by 'Non intuitive' to better explain what AI is really about. After all, as humans, we don't perceive ultrasound, infrared, X-Ray, Magnetism, Radio waves and other forms of Electro-Magnetic radiation, but still go ahead using and benefiting from them and not question them because the devices we use to benefit from such phenomena is 'artificial' (constructed by humans). Similarly, the technology of AI is trying to harness data relationship, patterns, inherent co-relationship which is not evident to the normal or super human eye (or intelligence). There is indeed sound mathematics (specifically, matrix multiplication, partial derivatives, linear algebra) behind AI. The real benefit of AI technology is to do tasks which are humanly impossible (or very difficult) at lesser cost. AI has indeed advanced to a level where it can beat any human in any strategy based game (e.g: Chess, Go, Shogi etc.). AI is way behind replace human empathy, creativity, love or hate, variety of physical skills, rational and irrational thinking. May be there is something else called 'Super Intelligence' that could over-ride human intelligence in another million years(or less). But the current AI shouldn't be confused for SI.

So, what are your thoughts about AI (using your NI)?
