The big (human) problem of overfitting and underfitting

One of the common problems discussed while working with AI/ML techniques is the problem of having a not so well balanced fit of a model, algorithm based on available data. Models, techniques are said to under-fit when the model has not learned well based on training data nor does it work well in a generalized manner. Over-fitting happens when model has learned even the noise in training data and believes that as gospel (it works very well with training data but fails with generalized data or test data). Though there are lots of techniques to overcome these problems, MLP(Multilayer Perceptron) Neural networks are typically afflicted with this problem of over-fitting.

On a tangential note, makes me wonder, if these MLPs are behaving exactly how we humans do! Many of us who exchange unsolicited messages and posts on social media are also afflicted with the same problem of under-fitting or over-fitting. Majority of us lack the patience to understand available history, documents, references, facts or do research but generalize and form strong opinion (low training, a generalization of everything we can perceive) and form under-fit opinions.

Then there are also so-called 'experts' who are biased with their ivy league degrees, social-cultural avatars and tend to over-fit every small geo-political event or the next tom-dick- harry they meet on the road.

So, next time you get that tantalizing tweet which makes your blood boil or a conspiracy theory which you can't resist from forwarding, just pause for a moment and wonder - is this a problem of under-fitting or over-fitting. Don't dismiss AI/ML as defective, they are simply mimicking a very common human trait !

Oh, I almost forgot what is called in human terms. Under-fitting is actually ignorance and over-fitting is called prejudice.
